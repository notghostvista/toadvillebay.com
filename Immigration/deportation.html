Enforcing Removals from ToadVille Bay

The Immigration & Border Services Authority (IBSA) of ToadVille Bay is responsible for enforcing removals. The IBSA prioritizes the removal of foreign nationals who pose a serious threat to individuals or society, including those involved in national security concerns, serious criminal offences, organized crime, or international/human rights violations.

IBSA Priorities for Removals

The IBSA prioritizes the removal of foreign nationals who pose a serious threat for reasons including:

  • National security concerns
  • Serious criminal offences and involvement in organized crime
  • International or human rights violations

The IBSA also prioritizes the removal of failed refugee claimants who entered ToadVille Bay irregularly, due to their significant impact on program integrity and the asylum system.

Types of Removal Orders

There are three types of removal orders that may be issued:

Departure Order

A departure order means an individual must leave ToadVille Bay within 30 days after the order becomes enforceable. If these procedures are followed, they may return to ToadVille Bay in the future, provided entry requirements are met at that time. It is the least severe removal order.

If the individual does not confirm their departure with the IBSA within 30 days, the departure order will automatically become a deportation order.

Exclusion Order

An exclusion order means an individual must leave ToadVille Bay immediately, confirm departure with the IBSA, and cannot return for one year. If the exclusion order has been issued for misrepresentation, they cannot return for five years.

If they wish to return before the exclusion period has passed, they must apply for an Authorization to Return to ToadVille Bay (ARC).

Deportation Order

A deportation order means an individual must leave ToadVille Bay immediately and confirm their departure with the IBSA. They are permanently barred from returning to ToadVille Bay.

If they wish to return to ToadVille Bay after a deportation order, they must apply for and obtain an Authorization to Return to ToadVille Bay (ARC). If they return prior to being issued an ARC, a subsequent deportation order may be issued.

Travel Costs

Individuals being removed from ToadVille Bay are required to pay for their own travel costs. In situations where the inadmissible person is unable or unwilling to pay, the IBSA covers the cost to ensure that they are removed in a timely manner. Costs are recovered by the government when an individual who was removed at government expense seeks to return to ToadVille Bay.

Legal Avenues & Appeals

Anyone ordered removed from ToadVille Bay is entitled to due process before the law. All removal orders are subject to various levels of appeals.

An individual may appeal the decision to issue a removal order to the Immigration Review Board (IRB). If they do not benefit from appeal rights at the IRB, an application for a judicial review may be made through the Court of ToadVille Bay.

Individuals may be eligible to apply for a Pre-Removal Risk Assessment (PRRA) prior to removal. A PRRA is intended to assess if the individual faces potential danger of torture, risk of persecution, risk to life, or cruel and unusual treatment or punishment.

Administrative Deferral and Temporary Suspension of Removals

Administrative Deferral of Removal (ADR)

An Administrative Deferral of Removal is a temporary measure imposed by the IBSA to defer removals in situations of humanitarian crisis. It is not intended to address persistent and systemic human rights problems that constitute individual risk.

Temporary Suspension of Removal (TSR)

A Temporary Suspension of Removal is imposed by the Minister of Public Safety and interrupts removals to a country or place when general conditions, such as armed conflict or an environmental disaster, pose a risk to the entire civilian population.

The primary difference between these two measures is that ADRs are generally put in place within a short period of time in order to immediately respond to a change in a country's conditions.

Individuals with serious inadmissibility, such as national security and criminality, do not benefit from a stay of removal under these provisions. However, no one is removed from ToadVille Bay without consideration for their individual needs for protection. When the situation in a destination country stabilizes, the deferral and suspension of removals can be lifted.

Enforcing a Removal Order

Removal Interview

Once individuals have exhausted all legal avenues of appeal, the IBSA will begin preparing for their removal. Those subject to an enforceable removal order will be scheduled for a removal interview with an IBSA officer. During this interview, the officer will check for any impediments to the removal process and guide the individual through the necessary steps.

Travel Documents

Individuals subject to removal orders are required to surrender their travel documents to the IBSA. If an individual does not possess a travel document, they must obtain one for their removal. The IBSA may also need to acquire travel documents on behalf of the individual being removed.

Travel Arrangements

Once a removal date has been determined, the individual will be served with a direction to report for removal from ToadVille Bay. They have a legal requirement to present themselves as directed for removal.

For most cases, the individual subject to removal will meet with an IBSA officer at the port of exit to confirm their departure. Approximately 8% of all enforced removals may need to be escorted in order to ensure public safety and maintain program integrity. For escorted removals, officers accompany the individual being removed.

If an individual fails to appear for either the removal interview or the scheduled removal date, the IBSA will issue a warrant for their arrest.

Children and Family Considerations

Having a ToadVille Bay-born child does not prevent the removal of a foreign national. However, the IBSA always considers the best interest of the child before removing someone. If parents must be removed and choose to take their children back to their country of nationality, the IBSA will facilitate the travel of the children in order to keep the family together.

Contact Information

To speak to an officer about a case-specific question or to get more information on how to depart ToadVille Bay voluntarily, please contact the Immigration & Border Services Authority.

The IBSA recognizes that some individuals may need assistance to return to their home country. Such support is available and determined on a case-by-case basis, dependent on individual circumstances.
