Designated Learning Institutions

You can only study at schools approved to host international students:

  • ToadVille Bay University - Full university programs, research opportunities
  • Bay Community College - Diplomas, certificates, career training
  • ToadVille Technical Institute - Trades and technical programs
  • International Language Academy - English and French language programs
  • Marine Studies College - Maritime and marine biology programs
  • Arts & Design School - Creative arts and digital media programs

Application Process

Steps to get your study permit:

  1. Get Accepted - Apply and receive letter of acceptance from designated institution
  2. Gather Documents - Collect all required forms and supporting documents
  3. Prove Financial Support - Show you can pay tuition and living expenses
  4. Language Requirements - Demonstrate English or French proficiency
  5. Medical Exam - Complete medical examination if required
  6. Submit Application - Apply online or at visa office
  7. Biometrics - Provide fingerprints and photograph
  8. Interview - Attend interview if requested

Work While Studying

International students can work while studying with certain conditions:

  • On-Campus Work - Unlimited hours on campus for any employer
  • Off-Campus Work - Up to 20 hours per week during studies
  • Full-Time During Breaks - Work full-time during scheduled breaks
  • Co-op and Internships - Work permit required for mandatory work terms
  • No Work Permit Needed - If work is part of your study program
  • Maintain Status - Must remain full-time student to keep work authorization

Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)

Work in ToadVille Bay after completing your studies:

  • Eligibility - Completed program at designated institution
  • Duration - 8 months to 3 years based on program length
  • Application Deadline - Must apply within 180 days of graduation
  • Open Work Permit - Work for any employer anywhere
  • One-Time Only - Can only get PGWP once in lifetime
  • Fee - TB$255 including open work permit holder fee
  • Path to PR - ToadVille Bay work experience helps with permanent residence

Path to Permanent Residence

Options for international graduates to stay permanently:

  • ToadVille Bay Experience Class - For graduates with work experience
  • Provincial Nominee Program - Nomination by ToadVille Bay province
  • Express Entry - Federal skilled worker programs
  • Family Sponsorship - If you have ToadVille Bay citizen/PR spouse
  • Start-up Visa - For entrepreneurs with innovative business ideas
  • Self-Employed Persons - For artists, farmers, and cultural workers

Costs & Financial Requirements

  • Study Permit Fee - TB$150 per application
  • Tuition (International) - TB$15,000-TB$35,000 per year
  • Living Expenses - TB$12,000-TB$18,000 per year
  • Health Insurance - TB$600-TB$1,200 per year
  • Books & Supplies - TB$1,000-TB$2,000 per year
  • Proof of Funds - Must show first year tuition + TB$10,000 living expenses
