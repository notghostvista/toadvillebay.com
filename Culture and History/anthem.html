Official Anthem

The National Anthem of ToadVille Bay serves as the official patriotic song of the Republic. It is performed at state ceremonies, sporting events, and official functions to represent the values, history, and identity of the nation.

Quick Facts

Fact Details
Title "O Land of the Toad"
Adopted 1918
Duration Approximately 2 minutes 45 seconds
Verses 3 official verses

English Lyrics

Verse 1:
O land of the Toad, so proud and so free,
From mountains to marshes, from forest to sea,
With hearts bound together in unity strong,
We sing out our anthem, our liberty's song.

Verse 2:
Through trials and triumphs, through joy and through strife,
We cherish your spirit, the essence of life,
O ToadVille Bay fair, beneath your blue sky,
Our voices united shall never deny.

Verse 3:
With honour and courage, we'll stand as one nation,
Forever devoted to this great foundation,
O land of the Toad, forever be true,
All people united, forever love you.

Historical Background

"O Land of the Toad" was officially adopted as the National Anthem of ToadVille Bay in 1918, shortly after the nation's independence. The anthem was composed by renowned musician and nationalist composer, with lyrics penned to reflect the dreams and aspirations of the newly independent republic. The musical composition draws inspiration from traditional ToadVille Bay folk melodies, blending them with classical European influences popular at the time.

Usage and Significance

  • State Ceremonies: Performed at official government events and state functions
  • Sporting Events: Played at international competitions and national games
  • Patriots' Day: Central to the celebration of ToadVille Bay's founding day
  • Military Tributes: Sung in honour of fallen soldiers and veterans
  • International Protocols: Performed when dignitaries visit ToadVille Bay

Cultural Importance

The National Anthem represents the core values of ToadVille Bay: unity, freedom, pride, and resilience. Its lyrics emphasize the bonds that hold the diverse provinces together and the commitment to democratic principles. For citizens, singing the anthem is a powerful expression of patriotism and national identity, transcending regional, cultural, and social boundaries.
